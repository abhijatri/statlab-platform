## Interactive Distribution Lab

<Intuition>
Every statistical model rests on a choice of distribution. This lab lets you
develop intuition for how distribution families differ in their shape, tail behaviour,
and parameterisation — the foundation for choosing models in practice.

Explore **18 distributions** across five families. Adjust parameters in real-time,
generate samples, compare tail heaviness on a log scale, and inspect exact moments.
</Intuition>

## How to Use

- **Left panel** — select a distribution family, then a specific distribution, then drag the parameter sliders.
- **Density tab** — side-by-side PDF and CDF plots, updated live.
- **Sampling tab** — generate up to 10,000 samples; histogram is overlaid with the theoretical PDF.
- **Tails tab** — log-scale PDF reveals whether the tail decays exponentially (straight line) or as a power law (concave); right-tail quantiles are marked.
- **Moments tab** — exact mean, variance, skewness, excess kurtosis, entropy. Undefined moments (e.g. Cauchy) display as —.
- **Compare tab** — overlay two distributions; adjust both sets of parameters independently.

## Distribution Families

### Classical

The workhorses of probability: Normal, Gamma, Beta, Chi-Squared, Exponential, Uniform. These arise naturally via the Central Limit Theorem, conjugacy, and order-statistic theory.

### Heavy-tail

Cauchy, Student-t, Pareto, Lévy, and Log-Normal. A distribution is heavy-tailed if $P(X > x) \sim x^{-\alpha}$ (power law). For Cauchy, **all moments diverge** — there is no mean.

### Extreme Value

<Definition number={1} name="Generalised Extreme Value (GEV)">
The GEV unifies three extreme-value limit distributions:
$$F(x;\mu,\sigma,\xi) = \exp\!\left(-\!\left(1 + \xi\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}\right)^{\!-1/\xi}\right).$$
- $\xi = 0$: **Gumbel** (exponential tail)
- $\xi > 0$: **Fréchet** (heavy power-law tail, used for financial extremes)
- $\xi < 0$: **Weibull-type** (bounded right tail)
</Definition>

The **GPD** (Generalised Pareto) governs the distribution of threshold exceedances — the basis of Extreme Value Theory in risk management.

### Advanced

Weibull, Laplace, Skew-Normal, Beta-Prime. The **Skew-Normal** $\text{SN}(\xi, \omega, \alpha)$ adds asymmetry to the Gaussian:
$$f(x) = \frac{2}{\omega}\,\phi\!\left(\frac{x-\xi}{\omega}\right)\Phi\!\left(\alpha\cdot\frac{x-\xi}{\omega}\right)$$
where $\alpha = 0$ recovers $\mathcal{N}(\xi, \omega^2)$.

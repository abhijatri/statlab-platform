# The Delta Method

If $\sqrt{n}(\bar X_n - \mu) \xrightarrow{d} N(0,\sigma^2)$ and $g$ is differentiable at $\mu$ with $g'(\mu) \neq 0$, then:

$$\sqrt{n}(g(\bar X_n) - g(\mu)) \xrightarrow{d} N(0, [g'(\mu)]^2\sigma^2)$$

## Proof sketch

By Taylor expansion around $\mu$:

$$g(\bar X_n) \approx g(\mu) + g'(\mu)(\bar X_n - \mu) + O((\bar X_n - \mu)^2)$$

Multiplying by $\sqrt{n}$ and using Slutsky's theorem:

$$\sqrt{n}(g(\bar X_n) - g(\mu)) \approx g'(\mu) \cdot \sqrt{n}(\bar X_n - \mu) \xrightarrow{d} g'(\mu) \cdot N(0,\sigma^2)$$

## Examples

**Log transformation** ($g(x) = \log x$, $\mu > 0$):

$$\sqrt{n}(\log\bar X_n - \log\mu) \xrightarrow{d} N\!\left(0, \frac{\sigma^2}{\mu^2}\right)$$

**Square root** ($g(x) = \sqrt{x}$):

$$\sqrt{n}(\sqrt{\bar X_n} - \sqrt\mu) \xrightarrow{d} N\!\left(0, \frac{\sigma^2}{4\mu}\right)$$

**MLE transformation**: if $\hat\theta \sim N(\theta, I(\theta)^{-1}/n)$ asymptotically, then:

$$\sqrt{n}(g(\hat\theta) - g(\theta)) \xrightarrow{d} N\!\left(0, \frac{[g'(\theta)]^2}{I(\theta)}\right)$$

## Variance-stabilizing transformations

Suppose $\text{Var}(X) = \sigma^2(\mu)$ depends on the mean. Choose $g$ so the asymptotic variance $[g'(\mu)]^2 \sigma^2(\mu) = c$ (constant).

This gives $g'(\mu) \propto 1/\sigma(\mu)$, so $g(\mu) = \int \frac{d\mu}{\sigma(\mu)}$.

| Distribution | $\sigma^2(\mu)$ | Stabilizing $g$ |
|---|---|---|
| Binomial proportion | $p(1-p)$ | $\arcsin(\sqrt{p})$ |
| Poisson | $\lambda$ | $\sqrt{\lambda}$ |
| Exponential | $1/\lambda^2$ | $\log \lambda$ |

After applying $g$, standard errors and confidence intervals are simpler.

## Multivariate delta method

For $\sqrt{n}(\bar{\mathbf{X}}_n - \boldsymbol\mu) \xrightarrow{d} N_k(\mathbf{0}, \Sigma)$ and $g: \mathbb{R}^k \to \mathbb{R}^m$ differentiable:

$$\sqrt{n}(g(\bar{\mathbf{X}}_n) - g(\boldsymbol\mu)) \xrightarrow{d} N_m\!\left(\mathbf{0}, J g(\boldsymbol\mu) \Sigma J g(\boldsymbol\mu)^\top\right)$$

where $Jg$ is the $m \times k$ Jacobian matrix of $g$.

## Second-order delta method

When $g'(\mu) = 0$, the first-order method gives a degenerate limit. The second-order:

$$n(g(\bar X_n) - g(\mu)) \xrightarrow{d} \frac{g''(\mu)\sigma^2}{2} \chi^2(1)$$

This arises, for example, when testing whether a parameter lies on the boundary of its parameter space.

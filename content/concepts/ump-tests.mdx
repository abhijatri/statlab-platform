# UMP and UMPU Tests

A **Uniformly Most Powerful (UMP) test** for testing $H_0: \theta \in \Theta_0$ vs $H_1: \theta \in \Theta_1$ is a size-$\alpha$ test whose power is at least as large as any other size-$\alpha$ test, **for every** $\theta \in \Theta_1$.

The Neyman-Pearson lemma gives the UMP test for simple hypotheses. For composite hypotheses, we need additional structure.

## Monotone Likelihood Ratio (MLR) Families

A family $\{f(x;\theta)\}$ has a **monotone likelihood ratio** in a statistic $T(x)$ if for $\theta_1 > \theta_2$:

$$\frac{f(x;\theta_1)}{f(x;\theta_2)} \text{ is non-decreasing in } T(x)$$

All one-parameter exponential families have MLR in the sufficient statistic $T(x)$.

## Karlin-Rubin Theorem

If the family has MLR in $T$, then for $H_0: \theta \leq \theta_0$ vs $H_1: \theta > \theta_0$:

**Reject $H_0$ iff $T(X) > c$** (where $P_{\theta_0}(T > c) = \alpha$)

is the **UMP size-$\alpha$ test**.

Intuition: larger values of $T$ provide monotonically increasing evidence for $\theta > \theta_0$.

## Examples of UMP tests

**Normal mean, $\sigma^2$ known**: test $\mu \leq 0$ vs $\mu > 0$ — UMP is $\bar{X} > z_\alpha / \sqrt{n}$.

**Poisson**: test $\lambda \leq \lambda_0$ vs $\lambda > \lambda_0$ — UMP rejects for large $\sum X_i$.

**Bernoulli**: test $p \leq p_0$ vs $p > p_0$ — UMP rejects for large $\bar{X}$.

## Why UMP doesn't exist for two-sided tests

For $H_0: \theta = \theta_0$ vs $H_1: \theta \neq \theta_0$, a UMP test would need to:
- Reject more often than the one-sided left test when $\theta < \theta_0$
- Reject more often than the one-sided right test when $\theta > \theta_0$

These requirements are mutually inconsistent (the two one-sided tests use opposite rejection regions), so no UMP exists in general.

## Uniformly Most Powerful Unbiased (UMPU) Tests

A test $\phi$ is **unbiased** if its power never falls below $\alpha$:

$$E_\theta[\phi(X)] \geq \alpha \quad \forall \theta \in \Theta_1$$

Among unbiased size-$\alpha$ tests, there often exists a **UMPU** test.

For the two-sided normal problem $H_0: \mu = \mu_0$ vs $H_1: \mu \neq \mu_0$ (with unknown $\sigma^2$):

The UMPU test is the **two-sided t-test**: reject if $|T_{n-1}| > t_{\alpha/2, n-1}$.

## Lehmann's non-existence result

For two-sided testing of a location parameter with a symmetric distribution, no UMP test exists. The UMPU test is the symmetric critical region that splits $\alpha$ equally. For asymmetric distributions or asymmetric alternatives, the UMPU test may use unequal tail probabilities.

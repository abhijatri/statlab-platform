# Exponential Families

The one-parameter exponential family in **natural (canonical) form** is:

$$f(x;\eta) = h(x)\exp(\eta \cdot T(x) - A(\eta))$$

where:
- $\eta$ — natural parameter
- $T(x)$ — sufficient statistic
- $h(x)$ — base measure (independent of $\eta$)
- $A(\eta)$ — **log-partition function** (normalization, ensures density integrates to 1)

## The cumulant-generating role of A(η)

The function $A(\eta)$ generates cumulants of $T(X)$:

$$E_\eta[T(X)] = A'(\eta), \quad \text{Var}_\eta[T(X)] = A''(\eta)$$

$A$ is always convex (because $A''(\eta) = \text{Var}[T] \geq 0$), which ensures identifiability.

## Examples in canonical form

| Distribution | $\eta$ | $T(x)$ | $A(\eta)$ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Normal($\mu,\sigma^2$) | $\mu/\sigma^2$ | $x$ | $\eta^2\sigma^2/2$ |
| Bernoulli($p$) | $\log\frac{p}{1-p}$ | $x$ | $\log(1+e^\eta)$ |
| Poisson($\lambda$) | $\log\lambda$ | $x$ | $e^\eta$ |
| Exponential($\lambda$) | $-\lambda$ | $x$ | $-\log(-\eta)$ |
| Gamma($\alpha,\beta$) | $-\beta$ | $x$ | $-\alpha\log(-\eta)-\log\Gamma(\alpha)$ |

## MLE via moment matching

For iid $X_1,\ldots,X_n$ from an exponential family, the MLE satisfies the equation:

$$E_{\hat\eta}[T(X)] = \bar T_n = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n T(X_i)$$

This is **moment matching**: set the theoretical mean of the sufficient statistic equal to its empirical mean. For the Bernoulli, this gives $\hat p = \bar X$. For the Poisson, $\hat\lambda = \bar X$.

## Sufficient statistic and completeness

For iid $X_1,\ldots,X_n$, the **complete sufficient statistic** is $\sum_{i=1}^n T(X_i)$. Completeness follows from the full-rank condition on the natural parameter space. By Lehmann-Scheffé, any unbiased function of this statistic is the UMVUE.

## Multiparameter exponential families

The $k$-parameter exponential family:

$$f(x;\boldsymbol\eta) = h(x)\exp\!\left(\boldsymbol\eta^\top \mathbf{T}(x) - A(\boldsymbol\eta)\right)$$

has Fisher information matrix $I(\boldsymbol\eta) = \nabla^2 A(\boldsymbol\eta)$ — the Hessian of the log-partition function.

## Conjugate priors

Every exponential family has a conjugate prior: $\pi(\eta \mid \tau, n_0) \propto \exp(\tau \cdot \eta - n_0 A(\eta))$, which is itself an exponential family. Posterior updates are additive: $(n_0, \tau) \to (n_0 + n, \tau + \sum T(x_i))$.

This is why Bayesian inference is so clean for exponential families — see the Conjugate Priors concept.

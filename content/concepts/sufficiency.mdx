# Sufficiency & UMVUE

A statistic $T(X)$ is **sufficient** for $\theta$ if the conditional distribution of $X$ given $T(X)$ does not depend on $\theta$. Intuitively: once you know $T$, the raw data add no information about $\theta$.

## Fisher-Neyman Factorization Theorem

$T(X)$ is sufficient for $\theta$ if and only if the joint density factors as:

$$f(\mathbf{x};\theta) = g(T(\mathbf{x}),\theta) \cdot h(\mathbf{x})$$

where $g$ depends on $\mathbf{x}$ only through $T$, and $h$ does not depend on $\theta$.

**Example.** For $X_1,\ldots,X_n \sim N(\mu,\sigma^2)$ (ÏƒÂ² known):

$$f(\mathbf{x};\mu) \propto \exp\!\left(-\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\sum(x_i-\mu)^2\right) = \exp\!\left(-\frac{n(\bar x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right)\exp\!\left(-\frac{\sum(x_i-\bar x)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right)$$

so $T = \bar{X}$ is sufficient for $\mu$.

## Rao-Blackwell Theorem

Let $W$ be any unbiased estimator of $\theta$, and let $T$ be sufficient for $\theta$. Define:

$$W^* = E[W \mid T]$$

Then:
1. $E[W^*] = \theta$ â€” still unbiased
2. $\text{Var}(W^*) \leq \text{Var}(W)$ for all $\theta$ â€” always at least as good
3. Strict inequality if $W$ is not already a function of $T$

The Rao-Blackwell theorem says: **never use an estimator that ignores a sufficient statistic**.

## Complete Sufficient Statistics

$T$ is **complete** if $E_\theta[g(T)] = 0$ for all $\theta$ implies $g(T) = 0$ a.s. for all $\theta$.

This is a minimality condition: a complete sufficient statistic contains exactly the right information, with no extra structure that could cancel out.

## Lehmann-ScheffĂ© Theorem

If $T$ is a **complete sufficient** statistic and $W = h(T)$ is any function of $T$ that is unbiased for $\theta$, then $W$ is the **unique** UMVUE (Uniformly Minimum Variance Unbiased Estimator).

Combined pipeline: find complete sufficient $T$ â†’ find $h(T)$ unbiased â†’ done.

## Exponential Families

One-parameter exponential families have density:

$$f(x;\theta) = h(x) \cdot \exp(\eta(\theta)T(x) - A(\theta))$$

Here $\sum_{i=1}^n T(X_i)$ is a **complete sufficient** statistic, and the Lehmann-ScheffĂ© theorem applies directly. This is why exponential families are so tractable.

## Ancillarity

The complement of sufficiency: $A$ is **ancillary** if its distribution does not depend on $\theta$ at all. Fisher argued that conditional inference should condition on ancillary statistics (Fisher's conditionality principle), though this leads to difficult conceptual issues (Basu's theorem).

## Historical note

Sufficiency was introduced by R.A. Fisher in 1920. The Rao-Blackwell theorem was proved independently by C.R. Rao (1945) and D. Blackwell (1947). Lehmann and ScheffĂ© proved the uniqueness result in 1950.

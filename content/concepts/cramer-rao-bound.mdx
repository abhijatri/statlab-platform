# Cramér-Rao Lower Bound

The Cramér-Rao Lower Bound (CRLB) gives the minimum possible variance for any unbiased estimator of a parameter, in terms of Fisher information.

## The bound

Let $T(X_1,\ldots,X_n)$ be any estimator of $\theta$ with bias $b(\theta) = E_\theta[T] - \theta$, assuming regularity conditions hold. Then:

$$\text{Var}_\theta(T) \geq \frac{[1 + b'(\theta)]^2}{I_n(\theta)}$$

For an **unbiased** estimator ($b(\theta) = 0$):

$$\text{Var}_\theta(T) \geq \frac{1}{n \cdot I(\theta)}$$

## Proof sketch

The proof uses the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality. For any unbiased $T$:

$$\text{Cov}(T, s(X;\theta)) = \frac{\partial}{\partial\theta} E[T] = 1$$

By Cauchy-Schwarz: $\text{Cov}(T,s)^2 \leq \text{Var}(T) \cdot \text{Var}(s) = \text{Var}(T) \cdot I_n(\theta)$

Therefore $1 \leq \text{Var}(T) \cdot I_n(\theta)$, which gives the result.

## When is the bound achieved?

The bound is achieved (an estimator is **efficient**) if and only if the score can be written as a linear function of $T$:

$$s(X;\theta) = \frac{\partial}{\partial\theta}\log f(X;\theta) = c(\theta)(T(X) - \theta)$$

This precisely characterizes the **exponential family**, which is why exponential families admit efficient estimators.

## Examples of efficient estimators

| Distribution | Parameter | UMVUE/MLE | Variance | CRLB |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Normal ($\sigma^2$ known) | $\mu$ | $\bar{X}$ | $\sigma^2/n$ | $\sigma^2/n$ ✓ |
| Exponential($\lambda$) | $\lambda$ | $n/\sum X_i$ | $\lambda^2/n$ | $\lambda^2/n$ ✓ |
| Poisson($\lambda$) | $\lambda$ | $\bar{X}$ | $\lambda/n$ | $\lambda/n$ ✓ |
| Bernoulli($p$) | $p$ | $\bar{X}$ | $p(1-p)/n$ | $p(1-p)/n$ ✓ |

## Efficiency

The **efficiency** of an estimator $T$ is:

$$e(T,\theta) = \frac{\text{CRLB}}{\text{Var}_\theta(T)} \in (0,1]$$

An estimator with $e = 1$ is called **fully efficient**. An estimator achieving $e < 1$ for all $\theta$ can always be improved by the Rao-Blackwell theorem.

## Asymptotic efficiency of MLE

Although many MLEs are biased for finite $n$, the MLE is asymptotically efficient:

$$\sqrt{n}(\hat\theta_{MLE} - \theta) \xrightarrow{d} N\!\left(0, \frac{1}{I(\theta)}\right)$$

No consistent estimator sequence can have a smaller asymptotic variance — this is the **Cramér-Rao efficiency bound** in the asymptotic sense.

## Common misconception

> "The CRLB doesn't apply to biased estimators."

False. The bound applies to *any* estimator, biased or unbiased — the correct form includes the derivative of the bias. A biased estimator can have variance below the *unbiased* CRLB.
